35th National Youth Scienceand Technology Innovation Competition

In the March of 2024, I participated in the 35th National Youth Science and Technology Innovation Competition with my research project titled
“Promoting Low-Carbon Campuses through a Water-Source Heat Pump District Cooling and Heating System under the Dual-Carbon Strategy.” This experience marked one of the most meaningful stages in my academic journey, as it allowed me to apply scientific thinking to real-world sustainability issues while developing technical, analytical, and communication skills.

The core of my research was to explore how new energy systems could replace traditional air-conditioning refrigerants such as Freon, which are major contributors to greenhouse gas emissions. By investigating the potential of a water-source heat pump system that utilizes river water as a thermal reservoir, I examined how renewable thermal energy could be integrated into campus-scale cooling and heating. To support my analysis, I used OpenStudio, an energy modeling software, to simulate the system’s performance and estimate the potential reduction in carbon emissions when applied to a school environment. Through these simulations, I learned to quantify how sustainable technologies can contribute to China’s dual-carbon goals—carbon peaking and carbon neutrality.

Throughout the research process, I gained valuable experience in using modeling tools and data-driven approaches to evaluate energy systems.Before this project, I had limited knowledge of energy modeling, but learning to operate OpenStudio taught me how to translate real-world conditions into computational models. I discovered the importance of setting appropriate parameters, validating assumptions, and interpreting data critically. More importantly, I realized that scientific innovation is not only about generating new ideas but also about supporting them with clear evidence and quantitative analysis.

Another significant part of the competition was the final defense session. Presenting my research to the judges and experts helped me improve my ability to communicate complex scientific ideas in a clear and accessible way. I learned to organize my arguments logically, highlight key findings effectively, and respond to questions with confidence. This process taught me how to bridge the gap between technical research and public understanding—a skill essential for future scientists and innovators.

Ultimately, my project was awarded the First Prize at the Jiangsu Provincial Level, which was both an honor and a motivation for me to continue exploring sustainable technologies. Beyond the recognition, the experience reinforced my belief in the importance of interdisciplinary learning. By combining principles from environmental science, thermodynamics, and data modeling, I discovered how engineering innovation can contribute to solving urgent environmental challenges.

edX:Pre-University Chemistry

Taking the edX course Pre-University Chemistry was an enriching and
comprehensive experience that strengthened my foundation in chemistry
while connecting scientific principles to real-world and emerging applications. The course was divided into three major sections: Energy
Transition, Chemistry of Health, and New Materials. Each part deepened
my understanding of different chemical concepts and their relevance to
both academic and practical contexts.

The first section, Energy Transition, focused on fundamental chemical
theories such as atomic structure, redox reactions, and chemical
equilibrium. Although these topics were partly familiar from my previous
A-level studies, the course presented them from a more analytical
perspective. I particularly enjoyed revisiting redox reactions, where I
could better appreciate how electron transfer governs both simple and
complex systems — from batteries to biological metabolism. The
discussion on chemical equilibrium also provided a bridge between theory
and experiment, helping me understand how microscopic dynamics determine
macroscopic stability. This section not only consolidated my knowledge
but also prepared me for deeper study in physical and inorganic
chemistry at the university level.

The second section, Chemistry of Health, was especially engaging because
it explored the chemical foundations of biological systems. I learned
how acid-base balance maintains homeostasis in the human body and how
intermolecular forces control the transport and interaction of
biomolecules. The exploration of biomolecules and macromolecules,
including proteins, lipids, and nucleic acids, offered a fascinating
intersection of chemistry and life sciences. It allowed me to see how
molecular structure determines function, and how subtle changes in
chemistry can lead to profound biological effects. This section reminded
me that chemistry is not isolated from life—it is the language through
which biological systems operate.

The final section, New Materials, introduced me to areas of chemistry
that are increasingly relevant in modern technology. I learned about the
properties of solids, metals, and the mechanisms underlying
electrochemical reactions. The discussion of electrochemistry helped me
connect earlier concepts of redox reactions with practical applications
such as fuel cells, corrosion prevention, and energy storage. The
introduction to nanochemistry was particularly inspiring; it showed how
the manipulation of matter at the nanoscale opens new possibilities in
materials science, medicine, and environmental protection. Understanding
how atomic structure translates into macroscopic properties made me
appreciate the creativity involved in designing new materials.

Pre-University Chemistry not only reinforced my theoretical background
but also expanded my vision of chemistry. It strengthened my analytical
thinking and experimental reasoning. Completing this course has deepened
my enthusiasm for pursuing further studies in chemistry and related
disciplines such as conservation science and materials research.

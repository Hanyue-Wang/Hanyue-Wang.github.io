2025-10-24

During the summer of 2025, I participated in the UKCHO China Camp, a chemistry enrichment program that offered me the opportunity to explore advanced laboratory techniques and deepen my understanding of chemical principles beyond the school curriculum. Over the course of the camp, I engaged in several hands-on experiments, including the extraction of shikimic acid, the determination of the solubility product of barium iodate, the synthesis of benzopinacolone, and compound identification using thin-layer chromatography. Each of these experiments strengthened my grasp of both theoretical and practical chemistry.

The extraction of shikimic acid was particularly fascinating because it connects organic chemistry with real-world pharmaceutical applications. I learned how to carefully control conditions such as solvent concentration and apparatus, which helped me appreciatethe techniques required in chemical manufacturing.

In the solubility product experiment of barium iodate, I practiced accurate titration and quantitative analysis. This experiment not only reinforced my understanding of ionic equilibria but also trained me to minimize experimental errors and interpret data systematically. It reminded me how chemistry depends on both conceptual reasoning and meticulous laboratory technique.

The synthesis of benzopinacolone introduced me to multistep organic synthesis and photochemical reactions. Observing how benzophenone transformed under UV light and later rearranged into benzopinacolone was a powerful demonstration of reaction mechanisms in action. It made me realize that organic chemistry is not just about memorizing equations—it’s about understanding molecular behavior and transformation logic.

Beyond the experiments themselves, the camp encouraged teamwork,
critical thinking, and scientific discussion. I collaborated with
students from different schools, exchanging ideas and solving unexpected
problems together. The instructors also guided us in connecting
experimental observations with industrial and environmental chemistry
applications, which broadened my vision of how chemistry contributes to
sustainable development and innovation.

Overall, the UKCHO China Camp was a transformative experience that
strengthened my passion for chemistry. It deepened my appreciation for
experimental design, data interpretation, and the creative process
behind scientific discovery. More importantly, it motivated me to pursue
future research opportunities and to view chemistry as a discipline that
bridges knowledge and application—linking molecular science to
real-world impact.
